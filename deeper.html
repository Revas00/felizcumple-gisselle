I think about you more than i want to admit You slip into my thoughts like you belong here. My body reacts to the memory of your voice and how you said that. But there are things i can't tell you with words, only show you while i hold you. I picture us, more than side by side, moving in rhythm, While we talk, letting the pause linger. Sometimes i imagine you here, not for visiting, but fitting. In my space, in my arms. Filling the silence with your sighs. More than just being here, belonging. Then i think about calling you mine. You being mine seems right. The gravity of you, it pulls me closer, even when i try to stay still. When i try not to feel too much. I want you slow, real, not because i need it, it'sbecause you feel inevitable. I won’t say anything aloud, not yet. But if you watch closely, you will see it.
You're not mine I know it's unfair we barely know each other, and I don't expect anything from you. But still, I can’t deny there’s something unsettling about the thought of someone else getting there faster. It’s not about possession, or love. And it’s not that I think I deserve it. I don’t. Not just any flowers. It's the kind you keep pressed between pages, wrapped in meaning, trembling with the weight of being first. You deserve a garden. You deserve someone who sees the roots, who waters slowly, who stays long enough to see you open. And I wanted to be that hand. not to rush you, but to be the first to offer something real. No pressure, no games. Just honesty and presence. not to impress, but to say: “I see you. I waited for this. You’re worth something that lasts.” I’m not trying to turn this into something it’s not. Maybe I’ll never get the chance. Maybe it won’t be me. But, there’s a part of me that wishes even if you’re not mine.
